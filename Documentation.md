## Book Choice & Justification  

The book I chose to create my website around is Pride & Prejudice (1813) by Jane Austen. I chose this book as it has been on my to be read list for awhile, and this task encourages me to read it. It is also a very well known classic. 
Pride & Prejudice is a classic romantic novel set in 19th Century rural England, revolving around Mr and Mrs Bennet and their five daughters. The eldest, Jane is optimistic, kind, and patient and known for her beauty. Elizabeth the second eldest, is independent, beautiful, playful, and intelligent, she is the main character in Pride and Prejudice. Catherine, Lydia and Mary are the last three sisters. Mrs Bennet is irrational, foolish and nosy. Mr Bennet on the other hand is intelligent, sarcastic but a weak and indolent father. Mr Bingeley is a wealthy, cheerful, polite and well-mannered gentleman. Mr Darcy is Mr Bingeley's wealthy, arrogant, intelligent, prideful but loyal friend. 

Pride and Prejudice is centered around the Bennet sisters need to get married for financial security as upon their father's death his estate is entailed to the closest male relative, Mr Collins. Upon moving to Netherfield Park from London Mr Bingley immediately falls in love with Jane at a ball, who returns the sentiment. Mr Darcy refuses to dance with Elizabteth or anyone outside his social circle, deeming her 'tolerable'. Mr Bingely's scheming sisters Miss Bingely and Mrs Hurst are not in favour of Mr Bingley and Jane's relationship. However, in later occasions Mr Darcy finds himself attracted to Elizabeth while she continues to dislike him. Throughout this time Elizabeth rejects Mr Collin's hand in marriage and becomes charmed by Mr Wickman whose stories influence her prejudice against Mr Darcy. Following Mr Bingely's later abrupt departure to London, Elizabeth believes that Mr Darcy is discouraging his relationship with Jane. Later Darcy declares his love and proposes to a surprised Elizabeth who rejects him. He demands an explanation and Elizabeth answers. Afterwards Darcy writes her a letter explaining the truth, which after reading, Elizabeth views him differently. The Bennet Family hears of Lydia’s elopement with Mr Wickham, threatening the reputation of the family and marriage prospects for the sisters. The Bennets’ are distressed, unable to find Lydia and Mr Wickham. Mr Darcy secretly intervenes and pays Wickham to marry Lydia and saves the family, Elizabeth finds this out. In the end, Jane and Mr Bingley get engaged and Darcy proposes to Elizabeth who accepts. Overall, Mr Darcy overcomes his pride and Elizabeth her prejudice in this novel of love, marriage and social status.

## Functional Requirements
The website will allow users to explore the characters and themes through interactive profiles. The website will be designed specifically to further engage the audiences with the book Pride and Prejudice. 
Users will have the option to select a character to view their backstory and appearance or choose a theme to further explore. 
When the user clicks characters, a screen will appear featuring profiles of each character. Users can select a specific character to view personality traits, appearance, backstory and general information. 
When the user clicks maps, a map will appear, with labels of each of the places the book explores.
If the user selects themes, they will be directed to a page displaying the themes of Pride and Prejudice. 
To experiment with the reliability of these features I will self-test and peer test. 

## Non-Functional Requirements
The performance between screens will be smooth by implementing one second navigation. 
The website will be accessible to all by using easy to navigate platforms and large fonts. To ensure the website's reliability I will be testing website performance across different types of devices. There are no privacy considerations to take into account as there is no need for a login. 

## Social Impact

The experience will be used by children and young adults (10+). Because of this the information will be written at a general reading level, with large fonts, and general images. 
Potential benefits include encouraging reading, deeper understanding and discussion within communities. It will also benefit students studying Pride and Prejudice for school projects. It will allow students to find in-depth analysis on information regarding themes and characters all on the same website. 
The book is set in the early 19th century however it seems oblivious to all the things happening around the world such as the Napoleonic War and the rapid spread of the industrial revolution. The book is mainly focused on the upper and middle class, failing to represent the lower class such as the servants. It does not show the reality and broader representation of people in lower, middle and upper class. Mr Darcy, also initially displays prejudice against the lower/middle class, assuming they are with less integrity and character than him who is in the upper class. He refuses to engage with any one of lower social class than him at the Meryton ball, only dancing with people in his own group. 
There are little risks regarding the website information, however there might be little risks to sensitive readers in the book. The book contains some alcohol/wine consumption at parties, themes of elitism and women’s reliance on marriage. There is a romantic elopement between Lydia and Mr Wickham as eloping and living together was scandalous if not married.There is manipulation by Mr Wickham who attempts to marry young, wealthy women such as Miss Darcy for their inheritance, he later lies to Elizabeth and manipulates Lydia.
