## Book Choice & Justification  

The book I chose to create my website around is Pride & Prejudice (1813) by Jane Austen. I chose this book as it has been on my to be read list for awhile, and this task encourages me to read it. 
Pride & Prejudice is a classic romantic novel set in the 19th Century rural England, revolving around the Mr and Mrs Bennet and their five daughters. The eldest, Jane is optimistic, kind, and patient and known for her beauty. Elizabeth the second eldest, is independent, beautiful, playful, and intelligent, she is the main character in Pride and Prejudice. Catherine, Lydia and Mary are the last three sisters. Mrs Bennet is irrational, foolish and nosy. Mr Bennet on the other hand is intelligent, scarcastic but a weak and indolent father. Mr Bingeley is a wealthy, cheerful, polite and well-mannered gentlemen. Mr Darcy is Mr Bingeley's wealthy, arrogant, intelligent, prideful but loyal friend. 

Pride and Prejudce is centered around the Bennet sisters need to get married for financial security as upon their father's death his estate is entailed to the closest male relative, Mr Collins. Upon moving to Netherfield Park from London Mr Bingely immediately falls in love with Jane at a ball, who returns the sentiment. Mr Darcy refuses to dance with Elizabteth or anyone outside his social circle, deeming her 'toleterable'. Mr Bingely's scheming sisters Miss Bingely and Mrs Hurst are not in favour of Mr Bingely and Jane's relationship. However, in later occaasions Mr Darcy finds himself attracted to Elizabeth while she continues to dislike him. Throughout this time Elizabeth rejects Mr Collin's hand in marriage and becomes charmed by Mr Wickman whose stories influences her prejudice against Mr Darcy. Following Mr Bingely's later abrupt departure to London, Elizabeth believes that Mr Darcy is discouraging his relationship with Jane. Later Darcy declares his love and proposes to a surprised Elizabeth who rejects him. He demands an explanation and Elizabeth answers. Afterwards Darcy writes her a letter explaining the truth, which after reading, Elizabeth views him differently. In the end, Jane and Mr Bingley get engaged and Darcy proposes to Elizabeth who accepts. Overall, Mr Darcy overcomes his pride and Elizabeth her prejudice in this novel of love, marriage and social status.

## Functional Requirements
The website will allow users to explore the characters and themes through interactive profiles. The website will be designed specifically to further engage the audiences with the book Pride and Prejudice. 
Users will have the option to select a character to view their backstory and appearance or choose a theme to further explore. 
When the user clicks characters, a screen will appear featuring profiles of each character. Users can select a specific character to view personality traits, appearance, backstory and general information. 
When the user clicks maps, a map will appear, with labels of each of the places the book explores.
If the user selects themes, they will be directed to a page displaying the themes of Pride and Prejudice. 
To experiment with the reliability of these features I will self-test and peer test. 

## Non-Functional Requirements
The performance between screens will be smooth by implementing one second navigation. 
The website will be accessible to all by using easy to navigate platforms and large fonts. To ensure the website's reliability I will be testing website performance across different types of devices. There are no privacy considerations to take into account as there is no need for a login.
